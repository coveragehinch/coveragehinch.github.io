Both are extremely useful, but very different products of wood and fire that have very different uses. Ashes are an excellent fertilizing material, containing all the minerals that plants mine from the soil, mostly potassium (potash) and calcium as lime. Ashes make slash and burn agriculture possible. Charcoal (aka biochar) has special properties that seem to be able to improve soils for centuries and may help in developing a more long term, sustainable agriculture.

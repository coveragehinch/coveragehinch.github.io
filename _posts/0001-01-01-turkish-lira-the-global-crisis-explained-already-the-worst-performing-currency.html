Despite Turkey’s government-reported 20% inflation rate, the central bank has been cutting interest rates against all conventional wisdom. The Lira meltdown is not some broad based “USD-Emerging Markets” matter- this is very specific to the idiosyncratic characteristics of Turkey, namely President Erdogan’s direct control over the central bank, and his highly unorthodox (i.e. completely backward) understanding of basic economics. More importantly, the lira is not isolated to Turkey - it poses enormous and largely unrecognized global contagion risk by way of the EU banking system’s exposure.

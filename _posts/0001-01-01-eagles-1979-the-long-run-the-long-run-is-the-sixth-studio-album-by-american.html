--- title: "Eagles - 1979 - The Long Run" image: "https:\/\/i.ytimg.com\/vi\/WkphbonxlO8\/hqdefault.jpg" vid_id: "WkphbonxlO8" categories: "People-Blogs" tags: ["Eagles"] date: "2021-11-18T09:57:07+03:00" vid_date: "2021-11-07T17:20:22Z" duration: "PT42M41S" viewcount: "1482" likeCount: "76" dislikeCount: "3" channel: "A Simple Man From 80s 2" --- {% raw %}The Long Run is the sixth studio album by American rock group the Eagles. It was released in 1979, on Asylum in the United States and the United Kingdom. This was the first Eagles album to feature Timothy B. Schmit, who had replaced founding member Randy Meisner, and the last full studio album to feature Don Felder before his termination from the band in 2001.

Three singles were released from the album, "Heartache Tonight", "The Long Run", and "I Can't Tell You Why". "Heartache Tonight" reached No. 1 on the singles chart and won a Grammy Award. The album was certified 7× Platinum by the RIAA and has sold more than eight million copies in the US.

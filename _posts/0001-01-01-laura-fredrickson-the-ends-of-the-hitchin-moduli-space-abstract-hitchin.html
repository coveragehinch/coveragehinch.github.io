In this talk, I’ll describe what solutions of SL(n, C)-Hitchin’s equations “near the ends” of the moduli space look like, and the resulting compactification of the Hitchin moduli space. Wild Hitchin moduli spaces are an important ingredient in this construction. This construction generalizes Mazzeo-Swoboda-Weiss-Witt’s construction of SL(2, C)-solutions of Hitchin’s equations where the Higgs field is “simple.”

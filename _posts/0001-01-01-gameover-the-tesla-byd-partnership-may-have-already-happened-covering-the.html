Tesla And LFP Batteries
According to sources, Tesla, the America-based carmaker, is shifting to Lithium Iron Phosphate batteries for all of its standard range electric vehicles. This is a massive step for the company, but it could prove highly profitable for Musk if it works out.
But why is Musk using LFP batteries? These batteries are known to be lower in cost and less energy-dense. The chief financial officer of Tesla, Zach Kickhorn said, "The primary exposure right now is around nickel and aluminum, nickel on the cell and aluminum on non-cell".
Another advantage of using lithium iron phosphate batteries is that they rely on raw materials abundantly present throughout the world. Unlike the previous ones, they don't rely on scarce raw materials like cobalt and nickel. According to sources, Tesla may have switched to LFP batteries to power a significant part of its fleet.

Government pays down your mortgage." image: "https:\/\/i.ytimg.com\/vi\/ArazWr_GYhw\/hqdefault.jpg" vid_id: "ArazWr_GYhw" categories: "Education" tags: ["mortgage forbearance","real estate","forbearance update"] date: "2021-11-03T23:05:37+03:00" vid_date: "2021-11-01T13:27:35Z" duration: "PT4M58S" viewcount: "5083" likeCount: "213" dislikeCount: "1" channel: "Motivational Esquire" --- {% raw %}Homeowners Assistance Fund contains a mortgage assistance provision that allows government money to be used to pay down homeowners principal. Federal rules allows for grants between $25,000 to $80,000, depending on the state, to be paid to eligible homeowners who are under the threat of losing their homes due to Covid-19. HAF, the $10 billion dollar fund, allows states to pay off a portion (or all) of a homeowners mortgage in order to make monthly mortgage payments more affordable. Attorney Diego G. Mendez, Esq, Miami, FL (Motivational Esquire) explains where to find the rule and how a struggling homeowner may be able to use this money to make their mortgage more affordable.

See the rule here: https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/coronavirus/assistance-for-state-local-and-tribal-governments/homeowner-assistance-fund

Corporate secrecy has long aided and abetted financial crime, enabling hidden company owners to carry out illicit activities without detection. These risks are being addressed via a recent wave of global legislative initiatives to create public registers of company ownership. In the Middle East, new rules are being introduced to increase the visibility of ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs). How effective are these changes likely to be and why is it so crucial for MENA-based organizations to understand who they’re truly doing business with?

The final installment of our Middle East Anti-Financial Crime webinar series explores the importance of corporate transparency and UBO compliance in a Middle Eastern context. Join us to hear a diversity of threat-based, regulatory and practical perspectives on the issue

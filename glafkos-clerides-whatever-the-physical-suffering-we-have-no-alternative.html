Turkish invasion of Cyprus | A divided Cyprus | This Week | 1974

CLERIDES INTERVIEW

(Reporter) "-If the Turks do virtually take over the
whole island there is nothing that you can do about it is there sir?

(Clerides) "-Militarily they can occupy the whole of Cyprus but they're not going to occupy it through a capitulation of us. We certainly we will fight and there will be come a time when
Mr. Ecevit's armorcraft will not be so usable because certainly we will
fight in the hills.

(R) -You're talking about guerrilla warfare?

(C) -I am talking about a long protracted warfare which will wage from the hills.

(R) -So are you saying therefore that it is quite impossible unless they retreat enormously to have
any kind of peaceful solution that the war goes on?

(C) -That is exactly what I am saying.

(R) -Does that mean that whatever the physical suffering of the Greek people
have to bear that you will fight them in the hills rather than that?

(C) -Whatever the physical suffering we have no alternative but to fight there are
certain commodities in life which even to secure physical comfort to not
constitute negotiable commodities and the freedom of one's people, the freedom of one's loved
is not a negotiable commodity"

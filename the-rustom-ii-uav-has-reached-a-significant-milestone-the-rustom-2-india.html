The Rustom-2 India's indigenous Medium Altitude Long Endurance Unmanned Aerial Vehicle has passed an important milestone and will be ready for production in two months.
The  (DRDO's) development programme has reached a milestone by reaching an altitude of 25,000 feet and an endurance of 10 hours,
And within two months, DRDO will demonstrate an altitude of 30,000 feet and an endurance of 18 hours that meets the requirements of the Services, said Dr. Tessy Thomas, Director General (Aeronautical Systems), DRDO .After the tests are completed in the next two months, it will be given to Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) and Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL) for production
In terms of future plans, Dr. Thomas stated that a programme for improved, more capable High Altitude Long Endurance (HALE) UAVs is also in the works.
The Rustom-2 MALE UAV is the latest addition to the Rustom series of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), which also includes the Rustom-I, Rustom-H, and Rustom-C
It is designed for use by the Indian Armed Forces, which include the army, navy, and air force, in intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance  missions.
In November, India's Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) flew a prototype of the Rustom-2 (Tapas 201) medium-altitude, long-endurance (MALE) unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) from the Chitradurga Aeronautical Test Range (ATR) in Karnataka, India.
The Indian Armed Forces tested the UAV's take-off, landing, and level flight capabilities during its maiden flight. Following the completion of design verification testing, the prototype will go through user validation trails.
The Directorate General of Aeronautical Quality Assurance (DGAQA) and the Center for Military Airworthiness and Certification (CEMILAC) have approved the Certification and quality assurance for the Rustom-II’s maiden flight

Why are Academics so critical of Luxenberg and Luling?

So, why do the Academics Dismiss Luxenberg so quickly?

But why make such odd claims such as these? Possibly it's out of fear of upsetting the Muslims in Germany, or in the countries where they still need to go to continue their research, or maybe it could be that their departments where they work are dependent on funds from the Muslim countries which sponsor them.

Whatever is the reason, similar areas of studies in other non-Muslim religious texts do not show such fear or hatred towards individuals who are breaking ground in new and exciting areas of research, suggesting that only in Islamic studies is this type of censorship evidenced.

Nonetheless, the younger academics who are now coming into universities in Europe and the US are beginning looking at Luxenberg and Luling anew, realizing that they have something special and substantial, which can't be simply dismissed with vitriol, laced with fear.

This video is all about what you need to do to take care of your plants during the winter months.

1. Watering: Reduce the amount of Water your plants are getting. Use a moisture meter to tell if the plants need water. You can bottom-water the plants in order to ensure the plants are watered when they are dry.
2. Temperature. Do not let your plants stay in temperatures below 55 degrees Fahrenheit. Senecio and Cacti do in fact do well when it is cooler, as they prefer to go dormant.
3. Humidity: Use a humidifier if necessary, place a vase or tray with evaporating water, or co-located your plants to increase humidity.
4. Light: Make adjustments to where you plants are and the exposure they are getting.
5. Fertilization: Be careful not to fertilize during the winter months, unless you see that they are growing, in which case you can fertilize just a little bit.
6. Pruning: Wait until spring to cut you plants back.
7. Clean your plants.
8. Control pests.

Considered the first woman private investigator of India, Rajani Pandit, has successfully solved around 88,000 cases, and written two books, which have won a total of eight awards. In her talk, she breaks all stereotypes that are usually associated with the profession of a detective, and shares her story and the incidents leading her to take up the profession. TEDxPaldi
Published by Rahul Parmar · December 23, 2018 at 12:59 PM ·
Rajani Pandit is an Indian private investigator who is regarded as the first woman private investigator in the state of Maharashtra, and sometimes the first in India.She has successfully solved around 88,000 cases in her career. Based on her experiences, Pandit has written two books: Faces Behind Faces and Mayajal. The former has won two awards, while the latter has won six awards.
In addition to this, Pandit is thr recipient of the Hirkani award from Doordarshan that honours women achievers. She is the subject of a documentary film made by Dinkar Rao, titled Lady James Bond. Her investigations are going to be made as a feature film named as Kuttrapayirchi. Actress Trisha will be reprising the character of Rajani Pandit.

text by Sara Teasdale (1884-1933). The narrator in the poem, realizing there is a beautiful world ahead with "sure stars shining" and peace as pure as "crystal," quickens their footsteps to that place where they will "make a world of their [own] devising" and find peace and "stars." There is a sense of euphoria and resolution to reach that utopian place.

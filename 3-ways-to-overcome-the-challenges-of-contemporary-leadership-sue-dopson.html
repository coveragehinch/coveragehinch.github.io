Sue Dopson, one of the Programme Directors on the Oxford Women's Leadership Development Programme from Saïd Business School, University of Oxford, discusses defining contemporary leadership and the challenges you may encounter.

Sue is a professor of organisational behaviour, a fellow of Green Templeton College, and deputy dean of Saïd Business School. She is a noted specialist on the personal and organisational dimensions of leadership and transformational change, especially in the public and healthcare sectors.

Are you looking to prepare for a leadership role in the future? The Oxford Women’s Leadership Development Programme offers you the opportunity to take a critical look at your own leadership style and strengths. Gain an understanding of the challenges female leaders face within the workplace and explore the unique strengths that will enable you to lead, negotiate, and influence others. Discover how to analyse and navigate cultural dimensions, and expand your mentorship capabilities in order to empower others to pursue leadership roles.

Overcome gender barriers and grow as a leader within your career and organisation.

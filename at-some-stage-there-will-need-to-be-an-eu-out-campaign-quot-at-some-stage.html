"At some stage there will need to be a single designated 'out campaign' and we will see who is part of it," the chair of Conservatives for Britain told the BBC's Andrew Neil.

Discussing the UK's current negotiations with the EU, Steve Baker MP said he would "love to back the prime minister" but made it clear that some MPs were preparing for an "out campaign" on the basis that early indications suggest their demands over the sovereignty of parliament in the UK would not be met.

Conservatives for Britain is a new group of 50+ MPs who want radical change in our EU membership terms - including restrictions on free movement of people and sovereignty for the UK parliament. However, Mr Baker said some colleagues who signed up to the group were "not necessarily committed to any particular red line."

On the 25th of September, 1978, Pacific Southwest Airlines Flight 182 was getting ready to land at San Diego International Airport. Its journey thus far had been uneventful. The day was bright and clear, the pilots were in good spirits, and the aircraft was functioning perfectly. Before it could safely come in to land, however, a tiny misunderstanding and a brief lapse in protocol would lead to an incident that would claim the lives of everybody on board, along with several others on the ground below.

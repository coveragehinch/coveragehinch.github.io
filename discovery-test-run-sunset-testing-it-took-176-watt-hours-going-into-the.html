It took 176 Watt-Hours going into the Inverter, to run the Charger, to refill Battery # 1 after this Run..., of which 26 W/Hr was used by the Inverter & Charger, and 7 W/Hr warmed the Battery from 15 Celsius to 23 Celsius, in going from 38.5 Volts to 41.8 Volts, over 2 & 1/4 Hours ; so 143 Watt/Hours actually went into the Battery and stayed there as recoverable Current....

The previous trip, taking the same route, but not using the Lights for the return-half..., and only using the "Pedal Assist" at "Level-9", took 135 W/Hrs Gross to recharge from ; or about 109 Watt/Hrs after deducting the cumulative inefficiencies...

So, running at full throttle and using Lights for half the Trip would mean a Range of only 17.5 Km from a 350 W/Hr Battery..., and my target is a 42 Km Round Trip....; so unless my # 1 Battery is a Dud, then one may have to aquire another pair of Spare Batteries, and thus be able to set out with one in the Bike & 3 more ready to play Swapsies - every 10 or 15 Km...

It isn't quite what I was expecting..., but at this point one might as well plough ahead despite such minor obstacles.

It's only money.

And the Goal is to be able to go to town & back on Stored Sunlight - without burning any Fossil Juice in order to do it.

Such is life,

Have a good one.

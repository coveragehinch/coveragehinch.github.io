Shan Noodles ("Ngiew Flower" Noodles) are a delicious treat to look for when you are in the North of Thailand. A dish that features several unique items, each of which bring a strong flavor to this 'Tai-Yai' (Shan) favorite; the dried sour/stinky bean 'cakes,' the dried-and-then-fried 'Ngiew' flowers, the soft and silky blood cake (sometimes pork, sometimes chicken blood), and last but definitely not least are the tomatoes.

This dish is amazing and it is THIS dish I want to introduce to you today. 'Khao Soi' of course is a classic, a favorite, a beautiful dish that does have its own roots right here in Northern Thailand (it doesn't come from here exactly, but eating it the way you see in this video today IS the local way and therefore, Chiang Mai can lay claim to the coconut-cream version of this dish).

So whenever you do have the chance to make your way up north, don't forget to keep your eyes open for "Nam Ngiao" Shan Noodles. Chiang Ria, Mae Hong Son, and of course Chiang Mai, all 3 famous Northern provinces have their own love for this dish, and all 3 of these are places where people of Tai Yai descent make their homes.

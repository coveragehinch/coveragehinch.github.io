100 Thieves announced the biggest fundraising round in history, raising 60 million
dollars and leaving the esports org Nadeshot founded 4 years ago valued a nearly
half a billion.

100 Thieves have just finished a 60 million dollar round of fundraising, the largest
private fundraising ever for an esports org...leading to Forbes now valuing the org at
nearly half a billion dollars...and leaving fans to ask if that money could be used to
get 100 Thieves into even more esports.

It also raises a question of just how valid that FaZe Clan valuation of 1 Billion dollars
when they went public really was, as a lot of journalists are having trouble believing
FaZe clan is worth more than twice what 100 Thieves are, especially after that 60
million dollar fundraising round. But will that 60 million get Nadeshot and 100
Thieves back into the HCS with a Halo Infinite team...or will Courage get his wish
and see 100 Thieves join up with Rocket League and the RLCS?

Both options are easily achievable with this huge influx of new money, and 100
Thieves has teased interest in both Halo and Rocket League, so hopefully those two
moves will now happen since they have an extra 60 million in the bank.

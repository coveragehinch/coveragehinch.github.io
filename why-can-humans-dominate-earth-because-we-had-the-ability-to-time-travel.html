Let’s get into today’s topic: Human time-traveling powers.
How did humans become the dominant form of life on Earth? We don’t have large elephant bodies, nor do we have sharp eagle vision. We don’t have strong bites like the crocodiles, nor do we have the speed of the cheetahs. We can’t fly on our own. Our body doesn’t have fur to keep us warm during winter. Our skin can be damaged by UV radiation. By no means are we the most powerful creature on Earth, so how did we come to dominate it? Some might say it’s because we’ve learned to use tools. But in nature, there are animals that use tools, just like we do. When a chimpanzee wants to eat ants, it uses a twig to poke into the ants’ nest. After the ants climb up the twig, the chimpanzee would eat the ant lollipop. Monkeys use stones to break open coconuts. A wide range of animals are considered to use tools. Some may then say that we have language. But animals also exchange information, such as through movements, sounds, or smells. None of these answers unlock the key to human’s domination of the planet. At this point you might be asking: Joseph, you make humans sound so useless, so why on earth can humans still rule this planet?

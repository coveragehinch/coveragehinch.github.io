Over the last few years - there has been a renewed demand to delete the words 'socialism' and 'secularism' from the Preamble of the Indian Constitution. The logic on offer is that these words were inserted by 'force' in the 42nd Amendment of the Constitution and are not constant with Indian thought and culture.

But even if that logic is accepted for arguments sake - will removing these two words alter our Constitution???? Is secularism and socialism limited to the Preamble or runs much much deeper in our Constitution. If that is the case - why is this game being played to remove these two words???The Deshbhakt takes a look at the games being played in #NewIndia.

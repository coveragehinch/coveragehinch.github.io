It is not clear whether the US government will offer Ghislaine Maxwell a deal, even if the disgraced socialite agrees to be a witness against other child sex traffickers, according to New York University’s Stephen Gillers.

Ms Maxwell is now facing the prospect of years in prison, after being found guilty on five out of six charges against her.

She was accused of recruiting and grooming four teenagers for Jeffrey Epstein to molest between 1994 and 2004 at his various properties.

It is alleged multiple powerful people may have been implicated in the child sex trafficking ring run by Maxwell and Epstein.

Professor Gillers said Ms Maxwell may offer to provide names of alleged perpetrators to authorities, however it is not clear if this would mitigate her sentence.

“She did not offer to cooperate to identify others,” Professor Gillers told Sky News.

“Now she might offer to be a witness against others in an effort to keep her sentence lower than it otherwise would be.

“Whether the government is willing to accept that deal is another question.”

The Minister of Defense, Nebojsa Stefanovic, paid an official visit to Turkey, during which he met in Ankara with the Minister of National Defense of the Republic of Turkey, Hulusi Akar. The two ministers discussed cooperation between the two countries in the field of defense and the possibilities of its further improvement, especially in the military-military and military-economic segment.
What could be expected from this cooperation is contracting new projects for joint production of complex combat systems and joint appearance on third markets, primarily in third world countries, ie countries in Africa and Asia that need quality, but more affordable weapons.
And what could be constructed and produced together with the Turks?
It is obvious that in the first place is the joint project of making the Lazanski armored combat vehicle, which is composed of the body of the ARMA 8x8 armored combat vehicle of the Turkish manufacturer Otokar and the Russian unmanned turret AU-220M with a 57mm cannon.
Lazanski is in fact an ARMA 8x8 armored combat vehicle with additional armor and a Russian weapon superstructure, so close cooperation with the Turkish manufacturer Otokar is necessary in order to complete this vehicle and translate it from a demonstrator into a prototype. The prototype would be subjected to detailed tests and tests, and its use value and tactical-technical characteristics could be confirmed by a technical testing center, all with the aim of obtaining a vehicle certification in order to enter serial production. Given the price of this $ 2.7 million armored combat vehicle with a complete package of equipment and additional armor, the big question is whether this vehicle will be purchased for armament in a large number of armored mechanized units of the Serbian Army or will be primarily intended for export and to some more powerful customers.
Certainly, when placing on foreign markets, the Turkish military industry could provide assistance to Yugoimport, because the Turks sell their weapons all over the world and have an extensive network of customers.
Ministar odbrane Nebojša Stefanović je boravio u zvaničnoj poseti Turskoj i pri tom se sastao u Ankari sa ministrom nacionalne odbrane Republike Turske Hulusi Akarom. Dvojica ministara razgovarala su o saradnji dveju zemalja u oblasti odbrane i mogućnostima njenog daljeg unapređenja, posebno u vojno-vojnom i vojnoekonomskom segmentu.
Ono što bi od ove saradnje moglo da se očekuje jeste ugovaranje novih projekata zajedničke proizvodnje složenih borbenih sistema i zajednički nastup na trećim tržištima pre svega zemalja trećeg sveta odnosno zemalja afrike i azije koje imaju potrebu za kvalitetnim, ali cenovno povoljnijim naoružanjem. A šta bi se moglo zajedno sa Turcima konstruisati i proizvoditi? Očigledno je da je na prvom mestu zajednički projekat izrade borbeno oklopnog vozila Lazanski koje je sastavljeno od tela borbeno oklopnog vozila ARMA pogona 8x8 turskog proizvođača Otokar i ruske besposadne kupole AU-220M sa topom kalibra 57mm.
Lazanski u stvari predstavlja borbeno oklopno vozilo ARMA 8x8 sa dodatnim oklopom i ruskom oružnom nadgradnjom, pa je s toga neophodna tesna saradanja sa turskim proizvođačem Otokar kako bi se ovo vozilo završilo i prevelo iz demonstratora u prototip. Na prototipu bi se izvršila detaljna ispitivanja i testiranja i čiju bi upotrebnu vrednost i taktičko-tehničke karakteristike mogao da potvrdi i tehničko opitni centar, a sve u cilju da vozilo dobije sertifikaciju kako bi ušlo u serijskou proizvodnju. S obzirom na cenu ovog borbeno oklopnog vozila od 2,7 miliona dolara sa kompletnim paketom opreme i dodatnog oklopa veliko je pitanje da li će ovo vozilo biti kupljeno za naoružavanje u velikom broju oklopno-mehanizovanih jedinica Vojske Srbije ili će pak prevashodno biti namenjeno izvozu i nekim platežno moćnijim kupcima.
Svakako bi prilikom plasmana na strana tržišta turska vojna industrija mogla da pruži pomoć Jugoimportu, jer Turci svoje naoružanje prodaju po celom svetu i imaju razgranatu mrežu kupaca.

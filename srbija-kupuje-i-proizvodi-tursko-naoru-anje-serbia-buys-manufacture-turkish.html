The Minister of Defense, Nebojsa Stefanovic, paid an official visit to Turkey, during which he met in Ankara with the Minister of National Defense of the Republic of Turkey, Hulusi Akar. The two ministers discussed cooperation between the two countries in the field of defense and the possibilities of its further improvement, especially in the military-military and military-economic segment.
What could be expected from this cooperation is contracting new projects for joint production of complex combat systems and joint appearance on third markets, primarily in third world countries, ie countries in Africa and Asia that need quality, but more affordable weapons.
And what could be constructed and produced together with the Turks?
It is obvious that in the first place is the joint project of making the Lazanski armored combat vehicle, which is composed of the body of the ARMA 8x8 armored combat vehicle of the Turkish manufacturer Otokar and the Russian unmanned turret AU-220M with a 57mm cannon.
Lazanski is in fact an ARMA 8x8 armored combat vehicle with additional armor and a Russian weapon superstructure, so close cooperation with the Turkish manufacturer Otokar is necessary in order to complete this vehicle and translate it from a demonstrator into a prototype. The prototype would be subjected to detailed tests and tests, and its use value and tactical-technical characteristics could be confirmed by a technical testing center, all with the aim of obtaining a vehicle certification in order to enter serial production. Given the price of this $ 2.7 million armored combat vehicle with a complete package of equipment and additional armor, the big question is whether this vehicle will be purchased for armament in a large number of armored mechanized units of the Serbian Army or will be primarily intended for export and to some more powerful customers.

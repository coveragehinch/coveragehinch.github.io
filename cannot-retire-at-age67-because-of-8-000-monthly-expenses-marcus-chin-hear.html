Marcus Chin, 67, Has To Pay S$5,000 A Month In Alimony & Child Support. Shares why he still lives in a rented room. When we see the headline, the quick conclusion is that he is broke.
$5,000 maintenance charges is an amount most people would find it hard to sustain. This is a good topic with learning points for all.

0:00 Some background on Marcus
He started his career in the 1970s and joined SAF music and drama company and subsequently SBC which was before mediacorp in 1973. Then, he was a budding singer.

Marcus shared that in 1970s and 1980s he was raking in big money. Per night, he would be paid USD$400 for Indonesia gigs and one trip he'd net easily USD$20k. That was in 1980s.

He was really a high income guy so the next question is where is the money?
Quite simple, he spent it all away, not on himself but with anyone around him. Bags/gifts – luxury life we see in celebrities who blow through their income

But it is not just that.
In 1990s he joined a MLM, at the peak he was raking in $40,000/month. That business folded because it violated commercial law . He was once again left with nothing.

3:16 FIRST LESSON: It doesn’t matter how much income, if you spend income you can be broke
The way to spend is spend investment return. If you get $10k from investment dividends go ahead and spend it, youd never be broker.

** General Knowledge Pub Quiz Trivia Questions And Answers:
1. Guess the country by flag below.
Netherlands

2. What is the rarest M&M color per pack?
Brown (13.5% of total)

3. What do you call a group of butterflies?
Swarm, flutter

4. True or false? Movie trailers were originally played after the movie(s).
True! They ‘trailed’ the movie. They were eventually moved to before the movie.

5. Nintendo Trivia: Who’s that Pokemon?
Psyduck

6. Solve the following math problem: 2^2^3
256

7. What do you call dried plums?
Prunes

8. Which NFL team has the most Super Bowl appearances?
The New England Patriots

9. Which African country was formerly known as Abyssinia?
Ethiopia

10. Who said this famous quote? “Two roads diverged in a wood, and I—I took the one less traveled by, And that has made all the difference.” ?
Robert Frost

11. Which country has the most vending machines per capita?
Japan (~4.1 million vending machines)

12. A tick bite can make you allergic to what kind of food?
Red meat

13. Which board game has 6 murder weapons? What are they?
Cluedo or Clue; rope, candlestick, revolver, lead pipe, wrench, knife

14. Disney Trivia: What does memorable Lion King phrase “Hakuna Matata” mean? And what language is it?
Swahili for “No worries”

15. Which singer has the real name Stefani Joanne Angelina Germanotta?
Lady Gaga

16. What is Pablo Picasso’s middle name?
Ruiz

17. What was the first toy to be advertised on television?
Mr. Potato Head

18. Monotremes are mammals that lay eggs and have no teats (produces milk from belly pores instead). Name the only 2 members of this group.
Echidna, Platypus

19. What do you call the tiny piece at the end of a shoelace?
An aglet

20. What is an “Onomatopoeia”?
Words that imitate sound effects i.e., “tick tock”, “oink”, “boom”, etc.

21. Disney Trivia: What are the names of Cinderella’s stepsisters?
Anastasia and Drizella

22. True or false? Hamlet is Shakespeare’s shortest play.
False. It is his longest play.

23. Guess the scrambled word below.
VELOCIRAPTOR

24. How many ribs are there in the human body?
24 (12 on each side)

25. Which horoscope is shown below and which months does it fall under?
Libra September 23 - October 23

26. Which U.S. State shares a border with only one other U.S. state?
Maine which borders New Hampshire and Quebec, Canada

27. Solve the following addition problem! 8989+5656
14645

28. Who said this famous quote? “A person who never made a mistake never tried anything new” ?
Albert Einstein

29. What do you call a group of tigers?
streak, ambush

30. What is the painting “La Gioconda” better known as and who painted it?
Mona Lisa, Leonardo da Vinci

31. What does the word “idyllic” mean?
Describes a extremely happy, peaceful, or picturesque setting

32. Disney Trivia: What is the name of Wendy’s dog in Peter Pan?
Nana

33. What is “Absinthe” and what color is it?
Green anise-flavored spirit

34. What is the world’s oldest forest and which country is it in? (Estimated 180 million years old)
Daintree Rainforest, Australia

35. Solve the following subtraction problem! 7231-232
6999

36. Who said this famous quote? “Everything has beauty, but not everyone can see.” ?
Confucius

37. Which actor plays Mark Zuckerberg in the movie “The Social Network” ?
Jesse Eisenberg

38. Guess the scrambled word below.
GRANDPARENTS

39. True or false? Oranges were originally green.
True! The Southeast Asian Tangerine-pomelo hybrid were green.

40. What does the word “Gerrymandering” mean?
Practice of setting boundaries of electoral districts to favor specific political interests

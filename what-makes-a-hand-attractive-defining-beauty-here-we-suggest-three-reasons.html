Here, we suggest three reasons why hands might be considered an important marker of genetic quality in men. These are: vascularity - the prominence of the veins, hand proportions, including finger length and symmetry, and, most interestingly, the 2D:4D ratio, which is the difference between the second and fourth digits (the first finger and the ring finger).

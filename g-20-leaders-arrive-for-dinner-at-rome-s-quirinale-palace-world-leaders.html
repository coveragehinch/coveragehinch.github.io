World leaders and other G-20 participants arrived at Quirinale Palace in Rome for a gala dinner hosted by Italian President Sergio Mattarella and Italian Prime Minister Mario Draghi.

The Group of 20 summit is taking place in Rome for the first time in person since the pandemic.

Italy's Premier Mario Draghi kicked off the conference of the world's powerhouse economies with a sharp call to pick up the pace in getting vaccines to poor countries, calling the gaping global COVID-19 vaccine gap "morally unacceptable."

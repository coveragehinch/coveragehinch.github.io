Weather, spelled, W-E-A-T-H-E-R, is a noun and a verb. As a noun, weather means the "atmosphere in terms of temperature, wind, clouds, and precipitation."

Examples:
I haven't seen the weather forecast. Is it going to rain tomorrow?
I don't like cold weather.

Weather can also be a verb. As a verb, to weather means "to withstand" or "to endure." It can also mean to wear away after long exposure to the atmosphere.

"Prepare to weather the storm," the sailors said.
The brick wall has weathered over the years.

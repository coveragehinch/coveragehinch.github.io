Antarctica has 70% of the Earth's fresh water frozen in a large ice sheet, about 4.8 km thick. Recently, On February 6, 2020, scientists have recorded highest Antarctic temperature. A temperature of 18.3°C in the southern polar region making it one of the fastest-warming places on Earth. The previous record was 17.5°C recorded in year 2015.

It has been found that in last 50 years the Antarctic temperature has risen by almost 3°C. Research has shown that if temperature increases by 2°C more it would cause a serious melting of ice sheets. Raising the world ocean levels by over 13 meters flooding several coastal cities and Island nations.

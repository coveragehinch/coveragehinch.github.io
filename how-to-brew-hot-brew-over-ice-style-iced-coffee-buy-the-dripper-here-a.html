The Hot brew over ice method of making iced coffee stands in stark contrast to vast majority of cold brew methods commonly used in cafes or by our guests when making coffee at home. The key difference being the use of hot water which allows this method to produce a cup that highlights the brighter fruit forward qualities of a coffee where as a room temperature immersion method for making Cold Brew downplays those flavors.

We love to use our Blue Bottle Dripper to celebrate our sinlge origin coffees in an iced format on those hot summer days and with this tutorial you can learn how to as well!

ou will need:
- Blue Bottle Coffee Dripper & Carafe
- 50 grams of Ice for carafe
- 20 grams of coffee
- 200 grams of water
- Coffee Grinder
- Digital Scale
- Pouring Kettle
- Glass with serving ice

Steps:
- Place 50 grams of ice into the Carafe
- Grind coffee on a setting slightly finer than a normal pour over grind setting
- Add ground coffee to the filter in the dripper and place on the carafe
- Start the timer and begin the bloom pour adding 40 grams of hot water, pouring 3 laps out to the edge and 3 laps back to the center over 10 seconds
- At 40 seconds begin the 2nd pour bringing the weight up to 120 grams using the same pattern as before
- At a minute 1 minute 10 seconds begin the 3rd pour bringing the weight up to 200 grams using the same pattern as before
- Let pour over finish dripping out and pour over fresh ice and enjoy!

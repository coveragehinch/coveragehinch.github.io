Many industries are currently being disrupted by a range of technological developments that are enabling new business models. From the sharing economy to robo investment advisors, remote health care operation to peer to peer payment systems, these developments are changing the ways firms connect with their customers or how they connect customers with suppliers.

Instead of a few episodic interactions, connections are becoming more continuous, with companies providing services and products as the needs of customers arise. Both incumbent firms and new entrants are trying to compete and reshape their industries by creating Connected Strategy (https://whr.tn/2tMrAGi).

Wharton Professor Nicolaj Siggelkow explains what Connected Strategy is, and how the topic is explored in his class "Strategy and Management for Competitive Advantage" at Wharton Executive Education.

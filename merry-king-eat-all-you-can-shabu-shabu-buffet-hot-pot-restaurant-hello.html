SHABU SHABU IS A KOREAN DISH OF THINLY SLICE MEAT AND VEGETABLES BOILED IN A WATER AND SERVE DIPPING SAUCE WHEN THE INGREDIENTS ARE STIRRED IN THE COOKING POT. IT IS USUALLY SERVE WITH TUFO AND VEGETABLES INCLUDING THE CHINESE CABBAGE, ONIONS, CARROTS AND MUSHROOMS. IN SOME PLACE NOODLES MAY ALSO BE SERVE. PUTTING ALL THE MEAT INTO THE THEN WAIT UNTIL IT COOK, THEN ONCE THE MEAT AND VEGETABLES HAVE BEEN EATEN, THE SOUP STOCK WILL REMAIN IN THE POT THE LEFTOVER BROTH FROM THE POT CAN BE CUSTOMARILY COMBINED WITH RICE. MOST COMMONLY MEAT ( BEEF, PORK, OR CHICKEN) IS USED FOR SHABU SHABU.
BUT RECENTLY THERE ARE ALSO INCREASING VARIATIONS WITH VEGETABLES OR FISH. IT'S TYPICAL HOT POT INGREDIENTS INCLUDE THINLY SLICED BEEF MEAT.

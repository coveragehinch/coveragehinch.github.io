On December 21st, the alert level of the Krysuvik volcano in Iceland was raised from yellow to orange. This occurred due to a series of several thousand shallow earthquakes whose epicenters were located on the Reykjanes peninsula. These earthquakes have a magmatic signature, indicating that a new eruption could begin once again on the peninsula. It may only be a matter of time before the famed Geldingadalir volcano erupts again or a new vent emerges.

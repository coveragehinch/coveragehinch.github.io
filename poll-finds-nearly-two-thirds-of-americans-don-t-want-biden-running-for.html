Joe Biden's approval rating continues to drop after a November poll found just 37.8 per cent of voters approve of the job he's done as president while 59 per cent disapprove.

The USA Today and Suffolk University poll surveyed 1,000 voters and found the Biden administration was continuing to see a decline in popularity.

It found 46 per cent think the president has done a worse job than expected which included 16 per cent who had voted for him in the federal election.

Vice President Kamala Harris' approval is also dire with just 27.8 per cent approving of her while 51.2 per cent disapprove.

It also found nearly two thirds of Americans – 64 per cent – don’t want the president to run for a second term in 2024.

The survey comes just one week after NBC News' poll found 71 per cent of voters believe the United States is "heading off on the wrong track".

"That is 21 points under water, he has dropped more in his first year than any other president since world war II in terms of polling," said Fox News contributor Joe Concha.

"This is a big problem and Kamala Harris at 28 per cent approval – she's supposed to be the torch bearer if an 80-something-year-old Joe Biden doesn't want to run in 2024.

"With those numbers somebody warm up Gavin Newsom on the bench."

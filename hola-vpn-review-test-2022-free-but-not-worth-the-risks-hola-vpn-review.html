Now, Hola is one of the better-known titles associated with VPNs. This service has amassed over 180 million users since its launch in 2012 and is a product of the Israel-based Hola Networks. It markets itself as the first peer-to-peer VPN on the market. So, How is it different than other VPNs?
Well, For starters, Hola makes users share their bandwidth with one another. As a result, You won’t be able to hide your IP address by connecting to a foreign server operated by Hola. In fact, You’ll likely be using someone else’s IP address, while another stranger will have connected to yours.
Of course, Hola is big on its peer-to-peer model. But this makes me question whether it brings any value to users, or instead, is it just a cheap move that benefits the company while exposing you to serious risks?
Now, Hola isn’t a traditional VPN by any means. It’s what is called a “freemium” service, meaning you can use it without paying anything. Then if you want, you can subscribe to the Plus package and pay for the extra features.
Unfortunately, The peer-to-peer model isn’t the only thing I didn't love. In my tests, Hola wasn’t reliable and I had trouble with some websites.
So is there anything that Hola does well? Let’s take a closer look and find out!
Okay, the first thing that I tested was its speeds, and to be fair it was decent. But here’s the thing – Hola VPN isn’t an actual VPN but rather a peer-to-peer proxy service.
So, with confusing advertising aside, this might be good if you’re mostly interested in speed.
Now, I tested Hola on a 75 Mbps connection. And The UK connection test delivered some great results. Of course, You’re not likely to notice a 1.2% decrease in speeds. Actually, the UK connection managed to unclog my upload speeds a bit! Because of this, I expected a decent showing from the U.S. connection as well. Again, The results I got were not bad at all, considering my test location is far away in Europe. So, Even though my speeds were cut in half, the U.S. connection was still good enough for hassle-free browsing.
Then, since I was Impressed with those e results, I checked out an Australian connection. Yes, I was hoping for a pleasant surprise. And, the download speed decreasing by 83.8% was expected because of the distance between locations. However, the upload speed dropped by 97.6% and was close to unusable.
Still keep in mind that these numbers will likely improve the closer you are to the connection source.
Yes, Overall, Hola was able to deliver great speeds. Which was Not a surprise, considering it’s a proxy tunnel without any encryption to slow it down.
